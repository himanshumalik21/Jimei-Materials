HTCC Technology Overview

High-Temperature Co-fired Ceramic (HTCC) is an advanced ceramic packaging technology that involves co-firing alumina or aluminum nitride green tapes with refractory metal conductors (tungsten or molybdenum) at temperatures exceeding 1500°C. This process creates dense, monolithic multilayer structures with embedded conductors, cavities, and feedthroughs.

The HTCC manufacturing process enables the creation of complex 3D structures with superior hermeticity, excellent thermal stability, and high mechanical strength. These packages are specifically engineered for applications requiring protection from extreme environments, including aerospace, medical implants, automotive sensors, and industrial controls.

Key Features & Benefits

Superior performance characteristics for demanding applications

Exceptional Hermeticity

Helium leak rate <1×10⁻⁸ atm·cc/s (MIL-STD-883 compliant) for complete environmental protection of sensitive electronics.

Extreme Temperature Stability

Operating range from -55°C to 800°C continuous with excellent thermal shock resistance (>1000 cycles).

High-Density Multilayer Integration

Up to 40+ layers with 100-150μm vias and fine-line resolution for complex 3D packaging solutions.

Excellent Electrical Properties

High insulation resistance (>10¹² Ω), low dielectric loss, and stable electrical characteristics over wide temperature ranges.

Mechanical Robustness

High flexural strength (400-500 MPa), hardness (15-20 GPa), and resistance to vibration and mechanical shock.

Material Flexibility

Available in Alumina (Al₂O₃) for standard applications or Aluminum Nitride (AlN) for enhanced thermal performance.
