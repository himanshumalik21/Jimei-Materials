Direct plated Copper (DPC)

Direct Plated Copper (DPC) is an advanced metallization process where a thin copper layer is directly electroplated onto a ceramic substrate, such as alumina (Al2O3) or aluminum nitride (AlN). This technique enables the formation of precise, high-density circuit patterns with excellent adhesion and uniformity. DPC substrates offer superior thermal conductivity, high electrical insulation, and outstanding reliability, making them ideal for demanding applications in power electronics, semiconductor laser heat sinks, thermoelectric coolers, and high-frequency devices. The DPC process allows for flexible design, fine line widths, and multilayer structures, supporting the miniaturization and enhanced performance of modern electronic modules.

Key Features & Benefits

High-density, fine-line circuit patterns Excellent adhesion between copper and ceramic Superior thermal conductivity and electrical insulation Flexible design for multilayer and custom shapes Stable performance in harsh environments Supports miniaturization of electronic modules Corrosion resistance and long-term reliability

Specifications

  • Ceramic Materials: Alumina (Al2O3), Aluminum Nitride (AlN)
  • Ceramic Thickness: 0.15mm to 1.0mm (custom thickness available)
  • Copper Thickness: 10µm to 100µm (custom thickness available)
  • Copper Purity: >99.9% Oxygen-Free Copper (OFC)
  • Surface Finish: As-plated, Solderable, Gold Plated, Silver Plated
  • Dimensions: Custom sizes up to 200mm x 200mm (other sizes available upon request)
  • Tolerances: ±0.03mm for ceramic thickness, ±0.01mm for copper thickness
  • Thermal Conductivity: Up to 170 W/m·K (depending on ceramic material)
  • Dielectric Strength: >10 kV/mm (depending on ceramic material and thickness)
  • Operating Temperature Range: -55°C to +200°C (depending on ceramic material)

With a comprehensive range of specifications and customization options, our ceramic substrates can be tailored to meet the precise needs of your application, ensuring optimal performance and reliability in even the most demanding environments.

Typical Applications

Power modules for inverters and converters Semiconductor laser heat sinks Thermoelectric cooler (TEC) substrates High-frequency and RF devices LED lighting modules Automotive electronics Medical and industrial sensors
